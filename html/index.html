Most modern smartphones, tablets, and laptops support eSIM. Popular compatible models include:

  • iPhones – XS, XR, and newer

  • Google Pixel – Pixel 3 and newer

  • Samsung Galaxy – S20, Note 20, and newer

  • iPads – Cellular models with eSIM capability

 

To check if your device supports eSIM:

  • Go to your device Settings > Mobile/Cellular

  • Or visit your manufacturer’s website

  • Or use this link to check: Device Compatibility

 

An eSIM (embedded SIM) is a digital alternative to the traditional SIM card. It’s built directly into your device—no physical card required.

With eSIM technology, you can:

  • Activate a mobile plan instantly using a QR code or app

  • Store multiple SIM profiles on a single device

  • Access local data rates while traveling abroad

  • Keep your primary number active while using Roamvy for data

 

It’s the smarter, more flexible way to stay connected—wherever you go.

To use a Roamvy eSIM, your device must be unlocked and eSIM-compatible.
Some devices may be locked if tied to a contract, and some older models may not support eSIM.

 

How to check if your device is unlocked?

You can:

  • Contact your current carrier for confirmation, or

  • Insert a SIM card from another carrier — if it works, your phone is unlocked.

 

Quick settings check:

  • iPhone: Go to Settings > Cellular > Add eSIM. If you see “Add eSIM”, your device is likely unlocked and supports eSIM.

  • Android: Go to Settings > Network & Internet > SIMs. If you see “Add mobile plan” or “Download a SIM”, your device is likely unlocked and supports eSIM.

 

How to check if your device is eSIM-compatible?

Quick method: Dial *#06# on your phone:

  • If you see an EID number, your device supports eSIM.

  • If you only see MEID (and no EID), it does not support eSIM.

 

Other methods:

  • Visit your manufacturer’s website for model-specific info.

  • Check our list here: Device Compatibility
